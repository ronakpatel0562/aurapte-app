--- Email 1: ENNA'S BIRTHDAY ---
Prompt: Your daughter Enna's birthday is next month. You are preparing an email invitation for all her elementary school classmates, and it will be sent to their parents. You should write between 80 and 120 words. Your ideas must come from the following three themes:
  • Date, time and location of the party
  • Food that will be served
  • Activities planned for the party
Sample Answer

Dear Parents,

I'm thrilled to invite you and your children to Enna's birthday party on Saturday, September 14th, at 3:00 PM. The celebration will be held at 123 Elm Street, Springfield.

We have a delicious spread planned, including pizza, sandwiches, fruit platters, and a variety of snacks and desserts to enjoy throughout the event.

The party will feature a treasure hunt, exciting games, and a craft station where you can create your own party hats. We'll also have a photo booth set up to capture all the fun moments and memories.

We are excited to celebrate with you and make this day truly special for Enna.

Best regards,
Alex Thompson

If an urgent matter requires my attention, I will be available via email at myemail@example.com. Please note that I will check my email less frequently.

Thank you for your understanding.

Best regards,
Alex Thompson

Best regards,
Jamie Lee
--- Email 4: ISSUES IN APARTMENT ---
Prompt: You are renting an apartment, but there are some problems with it. You are writing an email to the building manager/owner to complain that the neighbors above you are noisy and the hot water system is not well. You are writing an email to Marvin, the owner of the building, to solve this problem. You should write between 80 and 120 words. You should include:
  • What problems you have encountered
  • How you are affected
  • How to fix the problems
Sample Answer

Dear Marvin,

I hope you are doing well. I am writing to inform you about two problems I am facing in my apartment. The neighbors living above me are very noisy, especially during the evening and night. It is becoming hard for me to sleep, study, or relax at my home.

Also, the hot water system is not working properly again. This is not the first time it has happened, and it is making daily tasks like showering very uncomfortable.

I would really appreciate it if you could kindly talk to the neighbors about the noise and also arrange for someone to fix the hot water system as soon as possible.

Thank you for your attention.

Best regards,
Sarah Williams
--- Email 5: MOVING FURNITURE ---
Prompt: Your friend Jim is moving furniture this weekend. He originally asked you to help, but now your boss has asked you to work overtime to complete a task. So you're writing an email to explain why you can't help. You should write between 80 and 120 words. You should include the following reasons:
  • Express gratitude for the invitation to help
  • Apologize for not being able to assist
  • Provide reasons such as work commitments, transportation limitations, and family responsibilities
  • Offer alternative ways to help or support, if possible
Sample Answer

Dear Emily,

Thank you so much for inviting me to help with moving the furniture. I truly appreciate the thought and would have loved to assist.

I'm really sorry, due to work commitments, I am unable to be there. Additionally, I have limited transportation options at the moment and have pressing family responsibilities that require my attention.

I am really sorry I can't be there to help in person. However, if there are other ways I can support you, such as helping with packing or offering to coordinate with someone else who might assist, please let me know.

Thanks again for understanding, and best of luck with the move!

Best regards,
Jordan Lee
--- Email 6: MOVIE INVITATION ---
Prompt: Susan has invited you to watch a movie with her on Tuesday evening, but you can't go that time. Write a letter to explain why you can't go. You should write between 80 and 120 words. You should include:
  • Thank for her invitation
  • Apologize for it
  • Suggest another time to go
Sample Answer

Dear Ms. Susan,

Thank you so much for inviting me to the movie. I really appreciate your kind invitation.

Unfortunately, I have to decline as I recently lost my bag, which contained my wallet and essential items. This unexpected situation has caused a few complications, and I need to sort things out urgently.

I am really sorry for not being able to attend. Your invitation means a lot to me, and I regret that I can't be there.

I would still love to watch a movie with you! Would you be free on Thursday or Saturday evening instead? Let me know what works best for you.

Looking forward to spending time together soon.

Best regards,
Alex Thompson
--- Email 7: LOST BAG ---
Prompt: You and your friends had dinner at a restaurant yesterday. You think you left a bag at the restaurant. Write an email to the restaurant manager. You should write between 80 and 120 words. Your ideas must come from the following three themes:
  • Describe your situation: who you were with, where you sat, etc.
  • What the bag looks like and what was in the bag
  • Tell them how to contact you

Dear Manager,

I hope you are doing well. My friends and I had dinner at your restaurant yesterday evening around 7 PM. We were sitting at a table near the window, close to the entrance. After we left, I realized that I may have forgotten my bag at the restaurant.

The bag is a small black backpack with a zip pocket in the front. Inside the bag, there was a notebook, a phone charger, a small water bottle, and some personal items.

Could you please check if someone found it? If you find it, you can contact me at +1-555-234-6789 or email me at sara.james@email.com.

Thank you for your help.

Best regards,
Sara James

--- Email 9: PRESENTATION ---

Also, may I know the expected number of participants? This will help me prepare handouts.

Looking forward to your reply.

Best regards,
Tom Cruise
--- Email 10: ENERGY USAGE ---
Prompt: Your company is trying to save energy, and you want to write an email to make some suggestions to the manager Mrs. Smith about energy reduction. You should write between 80 and 120 words. Your ideas must come from the following three themes:
  • Turning off lights
  • Shutting down computers
  • Air conditioners/heaters
Sample Answer

Dear Mrs. Smith,

I hope you're doing well. I'd like to share a few ideas to help reduce energy use in our office.

First, we could encourage everyone to switch off lights in unused rooms, especially in less-frequented areas. This small habit could save a noticeable amount of electricity over time.

Second, staff should be reminded to shut down computers at the end of the day to avoid unnecessary energy consumption overnight.

Lastly, we could ask colleagues to adjust air conditioners or heaters according to the season and turn them off when not needed.

These simple steps can have a meaningful impact. Thank you for considering these suggestions.

Best regards,
James Anderson
--- Email 11: POOR LIGHTING IN BUILDING ---
Prompt: You live on the second floor of an apartment building with no elevator. There is only one weak light bulb at the entrance of the building and there is no light by the stairs. Please write an email to express these difficulties. You should write at least 100 words. Your ideas must come from the following three themes:
  • Install more lights
  • Brighter lights
  • Motion sensing lighting
Sample Answer

Dear Mr. Thompson,

I hope you are doing well. I am writing to share a concern about the lighting in our apartment building. I live on the second floor, and it is very dark at night.

There is only one small light at the entrance, and it does not give enough light. It is hard to see clearly, which can be unsafe.

There are also no lights near the stairs. This makes it difficult to walk up, especially when carrying bags or packages.

Could we please have brighter lights and install more along the stairs? Motion-sensing lights would also help and save electricity.

Thank you for your time and support.

Best regards,
Emily Carter
--- Email 12: NEW NEIGHBOR ---

Lastly, some garbage bags were left outside for several days, which can attract pests.

I'm sure these are easy to fix. Thank you for your understanding, and I look forward to getting to know you better.

Best regards,
Sarah Williams

--- Email 14: TIME OFF ---
Prompt: You are a student at a university. Write an email to your professor requesting some time off due to health issues. In your email, you should include:
  • Why you are requesting leave
  • Details about your health condition
  • How many extra days you need to complete the assignment
  • Appreciation for the professor's understanding and support
Sample Answer

Dear Professor,

I hope you are doing well. I am writing this email to request some time off because of my health condition.

For the last few days, I have been suffering from fever and weakness. Due to this, I am unable to attend my classes properly and focus on my assignment.

I kindly request you to give me an extension for completing the assignment. I need around five more days to finish my work carefully.

I assure you that I will submit the assignment as soon as possible after recovering.

Thank you very much for your understanding and support. I am really thankful to you for your kindness.

Yours sincerely,
Anna Johnson
